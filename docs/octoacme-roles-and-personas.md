# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
The Scrum Master facilitates agile ceremonies, removes impediments, and coaches the team in agile practices. They serve as a servant-leader who ensures the team can work effectively within an agile framework.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, daily standups, sprint reviews, and retrospectives
- Identify and remove blockers and impediments that hinder team progress
- Coach team members on agile principles and practices
- Shield the team from external distractions and scope creep
- Track and communicate team velocity and sprint health

### Goals
- Enable continuous delivery and incremental improvement
- Foster a culture of transparency, inspection, and adaptation
- Improve team collaboration and self-organization

### Typical Communication
- Daily standup facilitation
- Coordination with Project Manager on timeline and delivery risks
- Collaboration with Product Owner to maintain a healthy, prioritized backlog
- Retrospective action tracking and follow-up

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Project Manager**: Aligns on delivery timelines, escalates organizational impediments, and supports risk management
- **Product Owner / Product Manager**: Ensures backlog refinement sessions are productive and that sprint goals are clearly defined

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
The QA Lead coordinates testing plans, oversees bug tracking, and ensures quality gates are met before release. They champion quality standards across the entire development lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain testing strategies, plans, and quality standards
- Coordinate manual and automated testing activities across teams
- Track, triage, and prioritize defects in the bug tracking system
- Establish and enforce quality gates for release readiness
- Identify systemic quality issues and drive root cause analysis

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production
- Increase test coverage and reduce regression risk
- Ensure product quality aligns with customer expectations and acceptance criteria

### Typical Communication
- Test plan reviews with Developers and Project Managers
- Bug triage and status reports shared with the broader team
- Pre-release quality sign-off communications with the Release Manager

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Developers**: Collaborates on testability, code coverage, and defect resolution
- **Release Manager**: Provides quality gate sign-off and highlights risks before deployment
- **Product Manager**: Validates acceptance criteria and ensures delivered features meet business requirements

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
The Business Analyst gathers and clarifies requirements, bridges the gap between business stakeholders and technical teams, and validates that solutions align with business goals.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit, document, and validate business requirements with stakeholders
- Translate business needs into clear user stories and acceptance criteria
- Analyze workflows and identify process improvement opportunities
- Support the Product Manager in backlog grooming and prioritization
- Validate that delivered solutions meet business objectives

### Goals
- Ensure clear, complete, and actionable requirements reach the development team
- Reduce rework caused by misunderstood or incomplete specifications
- Align technical solutions with business strategy and user needs

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops and interviews with stakeholders
- User story documentation and acceptance criteria refinement sessions
- Solution validation reviews with development teams and Product Manager

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Product Manager**: Partners on defining and refining product requirements and prioritization
- **Developers**: Clarifies requirements, answers questions, and validates implemented solutions
- **Project Manager**: Supports scope management and change control by providing clear requirement documentation

---

## Customer Advocate

### Role Summary
The Customer Advocate represents end-user perspectives within the project team, ensuring that customer feedback is incorporated into the development process and that delivered solutions are customer-centric.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and synthesize customer feedback from various channels
- Represent end-user needs and pain points in planning and design discussions
- Evaluate features and documentation from a usability and customer experience perspective
- Ensure feedback loops are built into process documentation and workflows
- Champion accessibility and inclusive design considerations

### Goals
- Ensure the team builds solutions that genuinely solve customer problems
- Improve user satisfaction and product adoption
- Close the gap between internal assumptions and real customer expectations

### Typical Communication
- Regular feedback summaries shared with Product Manager and development teams
- Usability review sessions during sprint reviews and design phases
- Input on release notes and customer-facing documentation

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Product Manager**: Provides customer insights to inform roadmap prioritization and feature definition
- **Developers**: Advises on usability, accessibility, and customer experience during implementation
- **Project Manager**: Highlights customer impact when assessing project risks and trade-offs
