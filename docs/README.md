# OctoAcme Project Management Documentation Hub

Welcome to the central documentation hub for OctoAcme's project management processes. This folder contains all process documents, versioned workflows, and best practices that guide how OctoAcme teams plan, execute, and continuously improve their projects.

## Process Summary

OctoAcme operates on a structured, customer-first project lifecycle that emphasizes iterative delivery, clear ownership, and data-driven decisions. The organization follows five core phases: **Initiation** (validating business needs and stakeholder alignment through a lightweight one-pager), **Planning** (breaking work into shippable increments with prioritized backlogs and defined acceptance criteria), **Execution** (day-to-day delivery with daily standups, weekly syncs, and continuous testing), **Release** (standardized deployment with pre-release checklists and rollback plans), and **Closure & Retrospectives** (capturing learnings to drive continuous improvement). This end-to-end approach ensures consistent, repeatable project execution while reducing risk and single-person dependency.

OctoAcme's organizational structure defines three primary roles with complementary responsibilities: **Project Managers** coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and stakeholder communications to ensure projects stay on track; **Product Managers** define customer value, prioritize the backlog based on data and user research, and measure success against established metrics; and **Developers** implement features, write tests, participate in code reviews, and collaborate with leadership on design and technical decisions. This clearly defined accountability ensures smooth cross-functional collaboration and aligns teams around shared objectives.

Quality and risk management are embedded throughout OctoAcme's execution workflow. The organization maintains a risk register throughout the project lifecycle (identifying, assessing, mitigating, and monitoring risks), implements multi-layered quality gates including unit tests, integration tests, security scanning, and end-to-end smoke tests before release, and practices blocker escalation at three levels (team-level triage, PM/Product Lead involvement, and sponsor escalation for business-impacting issues). This proactive approach to risk and quality ensures projects meet their success criteria while maintaining psychological safety for continuous feedback and learning.

Communication is formalized through regular cadences and templates that maintain transparency and alignment across all stakeholders. OctoAcme teams conduct daily standups (15 minutes), weekly delivery syncs with demos, weekly PM/PdM alignment meetings, and monthly stakeholder updates, complemented by structured communication templates for weekly status reports and incident response. The organization uses a single source of truth (project README, release docs, and risk registers) and employs clear escalation paths to ensure decisions are made efficiently and information flows to the right stakeholders at the right time.
